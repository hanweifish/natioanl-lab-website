Description of recent research activities
( up to 200 words)
Our group is interested in understanding of light interaction with artificial microstructured materials, the development and applications of such microstructured materials as well. Both theoretical and experimental investigations are being conducted. Recent work is mostly on surface plasmon excitations in metal nanoparticles and metal nanostructures. The self- assembly-based techniques developed in our laboratory allow us to fabricate monodisperse metal nanoparticles and highly ordered microstructured metallic films with submicron spatial feature and large areas. The optical properties of these functional materials which we are studying include localized surface plasmons, plasmon-assisted photon tunneling through nanoholes, transition of localized SP to extended surface plasmon plaritons at percolation, magnetic response at optical frequencies, surface-enhanced Raman scattering using the materials as substrates, and others. We are also interested in the design and fabrication of photonic crystals with true photonic gaps. Examples are photonic crystals constructed from hollow dielectric spheres and metal nanoshells..
