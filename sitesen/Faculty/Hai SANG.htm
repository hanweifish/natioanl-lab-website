Description of recent research activities
( up to 200 words)
His current research interests include fabrication of nanostructured materials and the studies of their microstructural, magnetic, and electric properties; FM/AF bilayer with the dependence of exchange bias on the constituent materials and their thickness ; perpendicular anisotropy and extraordinary Hall effect in magnetic multilayer; and magnetoelectronics. He has taken on several programs supported by both NSF of China and NSF of Jiangsu Province of China. So far, he has published more than 70 papers in refereed journals. He was a visiting scientist doing his research in the Department of Physics and Astronomy of the Johns Hopkins University in 1997 and 2002. Now, he is a deputy director of National Lab of Solid State Microstructures of Nanjing University.
