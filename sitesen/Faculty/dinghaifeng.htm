Description of recent research activities
( up to 200 words)
1.Spin polarized scanning tunneling microscopy
Spin polarized scanning tunneling microscopy (SPSTM) is a powerful tool to image magnetic structures in real space with the resolution down to atomic scale. The technique is based on the tunneling magnetoresistance effect between a magnetic tip and a magnetic sample. Providing its high resolution imaging capabilities for both ferro- and antiferro-magnetic structures, SPSTM is an ideal tool to study magnetic nanostructures and the conjunctions of ferro-antiferro magnetic materials.
2.Properties of magnetic nanostructures
Nanostructures are the building blocks of novel functional materials and devices due to their rich properties. Here, we focus on the magnetic nanostructures as they offer the opportunity to manipulate the spins of electrons. Particularly, we create magnetic nono -lines, -dots via molecular beam epitaxy, explore the size-, dimension-dependent properties through low temperature SPSTM, in-situ Magneto-Optical Kerr Effect etc., and identify the underlining physical mechanism through the collaboration with theoretical groups.
3.Atom diffusion in quantum confined systems
Atom diffusion plays as an important role for the formation of nanostructures. Under the quantum confinement, atoms may diffuse differently. Our goal is to understand the atom diffusion in quantum confined systems, explore the new way for the control of the atom diffusion, and further the formation of nanostructures.
