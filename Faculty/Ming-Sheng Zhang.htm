Research area (up to 40 words)

 

Ferroelectric and optoelectronic physics, ferroelectric phase transitions, nonlinear optical properties, and laser Raman and Brillouin spectroscopy; focusing on the materials of oxides, perovskites, ferroelectric and dielectric thin films, and nanostrutured materials, ferroelectric liquid crystals and high temperature superconductors.

Description of recent research activities ( up to 200 words)

 

Research projects focus on third-order nonlinear optical susceptibility and nonlinear refractive index in thin films, SrBi 2 Ta 2 O 9 (SBT), (Pb, La)(Ti, Zr)TiO 3 and SrTiO 3 ; two-phonon absorption In thin films, BaTiO 3 and Ce:BaTiO 3 ; nonlinear interference in BaTiO 3 ,SrTiO 3 and KTaNbO 3 ; lattice and phase matching in optical waveguide Ba 2 NaNb 5 O 15 films on KTP;electronic state and size effects in photoluminesence of nanocrystals, SrTiO 3 , BaTiO 3 , TiO 2 and ZnO; size effect on phase transition sequence of TiO 2 nanocrystal; photo-induced light scattering and lattice vibration in Nd:LiTaO 3 , Co:, Cr: and Fe:LiNbO 3 ; phase structural anormaly and size-driven phase ransitions in Bi 4 Ti 3 O 12 nanocrystal and thin films; preparation, form mechanism, ferroelectric and optoelectronic properties in extrafine nanocrystals of TiO 2 and PbTiO 3 , PbTiO 3 nanorod, SnO 2 nanowire and ZnO leaf-shaped nanostructures; first principles calculation of electronic band structure, third-order nonlinear susceptibility and nonlinear refractive index in SrBi 2 Ta 2 O 9 , SrBi 2 Nb 2 O 9 , SrTiO 3 , Bi 4 Ti 3 O 12 and CaCu 3 Ti 4 O 12 (CCTO); two-photon absorption in BaTiO 3 ; size, stress and layer period effects on phase transition in ferroelectric thin films and ferroelectric strained-superlattice BaTiO 3 /SrTiO 3 .Published 170 papers.Received Progressive Award of Science and Technology (Jiangsu Province), Award of Chinese Association of Instrumental Analysis, Progressive Award of Science and Technology (Chinese Educational Ministry), and National Excellent Treatise Award for Best Paper.
