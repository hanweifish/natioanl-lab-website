Research area (up to 40 words)

 

Theory of ferroelectric and optoelectronic physics, first principles calculation of ferroelectric phase transitions, nonlinear optical susceptibility, nonlinear optical properties, focusing on the materials of oxides, perovskites, ferroelectric and dielectric thin films, nanostructured materials, and ferroelectric liquid crystals.

Description of recent research activities ( up to 200 words)

 

 

Research areas include first principles calculation of electronic band structures and nonlinear optical properties; Landau theory study for phase transitions in thin films and ferroelectric/dielectric superlattices; the subjects covering electronic band structures and third-order nonlinear susceptibility in ferroelectric and optoelectronic crystals, including the highly-attractive SrBi 2 Ta 2 O 9 , as well as SrBi 2 Nb 2 O 9 ; ferroelectric and nonlinear properties in BaTiO 3 , SrTiO 3 , Bi 4 Ti 3 O 12 and ABi 2 Ta 2 O 9 (A = Ca, Sr, or Ba) ; two-photon absorption in BaTiO 3 ; structural and electronic properties of BaTiO 3 (001) oxygen-vacancy surface; electronic and magnetic band structures in CaCu 3 Ti 4 O 12 , dielectric properties and layer periodic effects of the ferroelectric strained-superlattice BaTiO 3 /SrTiO 3 ; size-driven phase transitions and stress-induced diffuse dielectric anomaly in ferroelectric films; phase structures and stability in the ultrathin films; lattice matching and phase matching in optical waveguide Ba 2 NaNb 5 O 15 films on KTP; large third-order nonlinear optical susceptibility and nonlinear refractive index in thin film SrBi 2 Ta 2 O 9 ; size effects, photoluminescence and ferroelectric properties in nanocrystals, SrTiO 3 , BaTiO 3 , TiO 2 , PbTiO 3 nanorod, SnO 2 nanowire, and ZnO leaf-shaped structures.
Published one hundred papers. Awards include Progressive Award of Science and Technology ( Jiangsu Province ), Award of Chinese Association of Instrumental Analysis, Progressive Award of Science and Technology (Chinese Educational Ministry), National Excellent Treatise Award for Best Paper.
