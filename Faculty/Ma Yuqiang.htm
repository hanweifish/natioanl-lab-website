Description of recent research activities
( up to 200 words)
Our current interests focus on the study of soft condensed matter physics, with special emphasis on colloidal self-assembly and crystallization, p hase behavior in confined copolymers, and biologically motivated problems . The major tools we utilize to study these complex systems are self-consistent field theory, density functional technique, and computer simulation. In the area of colloidal assembly and crystallization we have developed a method based on the soft elastic substrates for stabilizing the ordering of symmetry-changing structures of colloids. In the inclusion-membrane complexes , based on several lines of experimental evidence for generic properties of lateral organization of inclusion-containing lipid membranes, we presented a general model system to explain some recent experimental findings and explore physical mechanism behind their formation. The model suggests a unifying mechanism for addressing the complex interactions between membranes and inclusions, which reflects the competition between the conformational entropy of lipids favoring the formation of lipid-rich domains and the steric repulsion of inclusions leading to the homogeneous dispersion. We are also particularly interested in the control and design of self-assembled ordering structures in confined copolymers, and emphasis the importance of entropy-driven ordering mechanism in polymeric systems.
